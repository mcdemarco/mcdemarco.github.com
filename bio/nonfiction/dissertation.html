Abstract

Since the introduction of logic programming in the hereditarily Harrop fragment of logic by Dale Miller et al., various semantic interpretations have been suggested, but they have been wanting in several respects: often the semantics have consisted of single term models unrelated to standard models for the full logic of which the language under consideration is a fragment; also, no one [see correction] has addressed the entire hereditarily Harrop fragment, either in the first-order or higher-order case. Thus no sematics is extant for lambdaProlog, the higher-order hereditarily Harrop logic programming language.

This dissertation includes an operational model for the first-order hereditarily Harrop fragment in the style of Miller, using Gopalan Nadathur's indexed-variable resolution method. A corresponding Kripke model is defined, and Miller's indexed model approach is thereby anchored firmly in the conventional semantics of constructive logic. This correspondence also yields a semantic proof of uniformity.

The remainder of the dissertation addresses the higher-order hereditarily Harrop fragment of constructive logic. On one front, Dana Scott's Omega-structures for constructive logic are combined with applicative structures for the lambda-calculus to supply a constructive model theory and cut elimination theorem for an intuitionistic version of Church's theory of types (ICTT). This necessary prerequisite to discussing lambdaProlog in the context of higher-order intuitionistic logic appeared to be absent from the literature. On another front, a class of models for λProlog considered not as a logical fragment but as an algebra of programs and goals is provided. One of these models is the higher-order version of the operational model.

Corrections

There is another model theory for the first-order hereditarily Harrop fragment of which I was unaware, in Evgeny Makarov, "Models of Logic Programs w.r.t. Intuitionistic and Minimal Logics," Proceedings of the 5th Workshop on Logic, Language, Information and Computation, São Paulo, Brazil, July 28-31, 1998, pages 126-131.

New versions of the models for ICTT and UCTT are forthcoming.
